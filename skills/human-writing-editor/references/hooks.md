# Hooks - openers that earn the click without faking it

Applies when content-modes says so (linkedin, social-post). The first 210
characters (before LinkedIn's "see more") decide whether the post gets read;
this file is the positive library for building that line. It complements
ai-tells.md: everything here must still pass those rules.

Sources: practitioner datasets (AuthoredUp corpus and others, 2025-2026) and a
curated subset of the 16-formula set from sergebulaev/linkedin-skills (MIT,
(c) 2026 Sergey Bulaev), adapted to this bundle's anti-slop register.

## The hard law

**A hook may only promise what the body delivers, built from facts already in
the draft.** Never invent a number, scene, or claim to make the opener stronger.
If the draft has no hook material, sharpen the claim it does have; do not
manufacture mystery.

## What the data says

- Best performers: specific-number openers (~1.7x vs plain opener), story
  cold-opens (~1.5x), genuinely contrarian takes (up to 2.3x engagement).
- Dead on arrival: question-only openers ("Ever wonder why...?"), imperative
  "Stop doing X" (overused into invisibility), hedging in line one ("I think",
  "maybe"), setup colons ("Here's the thing:").
- "How I [did X]" beats "How to [do X]": happened beats theoretical.
- Internally draft 3 hook variants from different formulas, pick the one that
  survives a cold read; output only the winner (plus alternates when the
  content mode asks for them).

## Formulas

Pick by what the post should earn, then by what material the draft contains.

### H1 - Specific number opener (earns: reach, credibility)

`[Number from the draft] + [the claim it proves].`
"73% of our signups never open the dashboard. The onboarding was the product."
Constraint: the number must exist in the draft. Odd precision beats round
("$4,217", not "~$4k") when the draft has it.

### H2 - Story cold-open (earns: dwell time)

`[Specific moment: time, place, person] + [the stakes, resolution withheld].`
"Last Tuesday a client cancelled a $14k retainer over one sentence in my proposal."
Constraint: the scene must come from the draft. Small and concrete beats epic.

### H3 - Contrarian with receipts (earns: comments)

`[Common belief] + [flat disagreement] + [the evidence type you're about to show].`
"Posting daily is the most repeated LinkedIn advice. Our client data says it's wrong for accounts under 5k."
Constraint: the disagreement must be genuine and defended in the body with
specifics. If the body can't back it, downgrade to H6.

### H4 - Time-anchor confession (earns: comments, trust)

`[N days/months ago] + [I stopped/started/was wrong about X].`
"Six months ago I stopped writing proposals. Revenue went up."
Constraint: first person only if the draft is first-person experience. No
performative vulnerability (see founder voice rules).

### H5 - Year-over-year / before-after pivot (earns: saves)

`[Old state, concrete] + [new state, concrete]. + [what actually changed]`
"In 2025 we shipped 12 posts a month and got 40 leads. In 2026 we ship 4 and get 90."
Constraint: both states need real numbers or named specifics from the draft.

### H6 - Reframe (earns: saves, quotes)

`[What people think X is] + [what it actually is, per the draft's argument].`
"Most teams treat retros as therapy. They're a backlog source."
Constraint: this is the safe default when the draft argues a point but has no
numbers or scenes. Keep both halves concrete; abstract-vs-abstract is beige.

### H7 - Named scenario (earns: relevance for a niche)

`[A person/company type in a specific situation from the draft] + [the tension].`
"A founder with 12,000 followers who can't book five calls a month has an audience problem, not a content problem."
Constraint: type-level naming is fine; never fabricate a real identity.

### H8 - Curiosity gap, constrained (earns: see-more clicks)

`[Specific true premise] + [the resolution the body genuinely contains].`
"We tested 5 hook styles across 200 client posts. The worst-to-best gap was 3.4x."
Constraint: the gap must close inside the post. If the withheld thing is the
whole product ("comment X and I'll DM you"), that's bait - banned.

## Never

- Comment-gate lead magnets ("comment KEYWORD below") - engagement bait.
- Anaphora stacks and rule-of-three rhythm openers (TELL-005 territory).
- Curiosity without a payload ("What happened next shocked me").
- A hook whose energy the body can't sustain - readers punish the cheque
  that bounces harder than a plain opener.
- Any fact, number, or scene not present in the source draft (the hard law).
