## EN · LinkedIn post

**Before (AI-ish, 7/10):**
> In today's fast-paced digital landscape, building trust is more important than ever. It's not just about transparency — it's about creating meaningful connections with users. Companies that leverage robust frameworks for trust-building unlock significant competitive advantages and foster long-term loyalty. The key takeaway? Trust is the new currency.

**After (operator voice, 1/10):**
> Most products lose users before users trust them. Transparency helps, but it's not enough. People need to understand what's happening with their money — what fees you take, when you take them, what happens if they want out. Trust isn't a marketing layer. It's the bottom three lines of your fee disclosure being readable.

**Changes applied:**
- Cut TELL-003 (generic opening).
- Cut TELL-002 (fake contrast formula).
- Cut TELL-006 (key takeaway).
- Replaced abstract verbs (leverage, foster, unlock) with concrete actions.
- Added named specifics (fees, disclosure).

## EN · LinkedIn post — heavy AI cadence (founder voice)

This case is the canonical "every individual sentence looks fine but the rhythm is pure LinkedIn-AI" failure mode. Source uses TELL-002 split, TELL-005, TELL-016 Pattern B, TELL-019 Pattern B, TELL-021, TELL-022, and TELL-023 simultaneously.

**Before (AI-ish, 8/10):**
> Your job is becoming training data.
>
> Meta is rolling out software that records how employees actually work — clicks, keystrokes, shortcuts, small decisions no one documents.
>
> Not to monitor.
>
> To replicate.
>
> At the same time:
> – up to 20% headcount cuts
> – massive AI spend locked in
>
> The loop is simple:
> watch → learn → replace
>
> You're no longer just doing the job.
> You're teaching the system how to do it without you.
>
> The exit interview isn't feedback.
>
> It's the final dataset.

**Tells firing:**
- TELL-002 (fragment variant): "Not to monitor. / To replicate."
- TELL-019 Pattern C: "At the same time:" + bullet list.
- TELL-019 Pattern B + TELL-021: "The loop is simple: / watch → learn → replace".
- TELL-022 reframe couplet: "You're no longer just doing the job. / You're teaching the system…"
- TELL-002 split + TELL-016 Pattern B: "The exit interview isn't feedback. / It's the final dataset."
- TELL-023: every paragraph is 1–2 sentences. Whitespace-heavy. No prose paragraph anywhere.
- Em dash in opening line.

**After (founder voice, target ≤2/10):**
> Meta just rolled out software that records what employees do at the keyboard. Every click, every keystroke, every shortcut, every small decision nobody writes down.
>
> The framing is "tooling improvements." I read it differently. The same year, Meta is cutting up to 20% of headcount and locking in record AI spend, and the recordings are training data for the systems that will absorb the work. The recording is data collection, and the job is the dataset.
>
> Every shortcut you take at the keyboard is a labelled training example. The exit interview is the last datapoint.
>
> I don't think this is a uniquely Meta story. It's the shape every large company with cash and an AI budget will move toward. If you work somewhere that suddenly cares about your workflow telemetry, that's the reason.

**Changes applied:**
- Killed TELL-002 fragment couplet ("Not to monitor. / To replicate.") — folded into the second paragraph as part of the prose claim.
- Killed TELL-019 Pattern C ("At the same time:") and the bulleted list — rewritten as one prose sentence with the same facts.
- Killed TELL-021 + TELL-019 Pattern B ("The loop is simple: watch → learn → replace") — rewritten as named-actor prose: "training data for the systems that will absorb the work."
- Killed TELL-022 reframe couplet ("You're no longer just doing the job. You're teaching the system…") — replaced with a first-person observation + concrete claim: "Every shortcut you take at the keyboard is a labelled training example."
- Killed TELL-016 Pattern B reveal closer ("It's the final dataset.") — collapsed into the previous claim: "The exit interview is the last datapoint."
- Fixed TELL-023 paragraph rhythm: introduced one 4-sentence prose paragraph (the second one). Mix of sentence lengths inside paragraphs.
- Added founder voice: "I read it differently", "I don't think this is a uniquely Meta story" — first-person stake without performance.
- Removed em dash in opening.
- Preserved facts: "up to 20% of headcount", "record AI spend", "Meta", "recording at the keyboard."

**Before:**
> Throughout history, technological innovation has played a pivotal role in shaping the trajectory of human development. **Changes:**
- Cut TELL-003 (generic opener).
- Cut TELL-007 (forced conclusion).
- Cut TELL-006 (importance phrase).
